§ Honest fit assessment

Is Component A right for you?

Component A is the highest-value KUSUM path but the most restrictive. Most farmers won't qualify because of the substation-proximity and land-type requirements. Here's the honest breakdown.

✅ Strong fit if:

  • You own 4-16 acres of barren or non-cultivable land (RoR / Khasra-Khatauni proves ownership)
  • The land is within 5 km of a 33/11 kV electrical substation
  • The land is currently idle, fallow, or uncultivable (rocky, saline, low water table) — generating zero income
  • You're comfortable with a 25-year commitment (the PPA is binding)
  • You're either an individual landowner-farmer, FPO, cooperative, panchayat, or water user association

⚠️ Not a fit if:

  • Your land is actively cultivated (Component A explicitly excludes productive land)
  • Your land is more than 5 km from any substation (transmission costs kill the economics)
  • Land ownership is disputed or held jointly without clear title
  • You need to retain full agricultural use of the land
  • Your land is in a protected zone (forest, ecological reserve, ASI heritage area)

Bundelkhand vs Eastern UP variance

Earnings per acre vary by location due to solar irradiance:

  • Bundelkhand (Jhansi, Banda, Lalitpur, Mahoba): ₹65-75K/acre/year (highest irradiance in UP, ~5.5 kWh/m²/day)
  • Western UP (Agra, Meerut, Aligarh): ₹60-65K/acre/year (mid-range, ~5.0 kWh/m²/day)
  • Eastern UP (Varanasi, Gorakhpur): ₹55-60K/acre/year (lower, ~4.6 kWh/m²/day, monsoon cloudiness)

⚠️ The DISCOM payment risk

You don't get this income from the developer — you get it from UPPCL paying the developer, who then pays you per the PPA structure. Some UP DISCOMs have been slow to pay solar generators in 2024-25 due to cash-flow issues.

Bundelkhand farmers under Component A have reported 60-90 day payment delays. The income is real and contractually guaranteed, but cash-flow timing is not. Plan for delays in the first 1-2 years until DISCOM operations stabilise.

§ The application process

From application to monthly income.

Total timeline: roughly 15-18 months from initial application to operational plant. Yes, that's long. Component A requires substation feasibility studies, PPA negotiations, and full plant construction — it's not a simple subsidy disbursement.

Step 01 · Month 1

Application

Apply on PM-KUSUM portal or UPNEDA office with land records, Aadhaar, bank details.

Step 02 · Month 1-3

UPNEDA verification

Land ownership + substation proximity (within 5 km) verified. Site feasibility study by DISCOM.

Step 03 · Month 3-6

Developer matching

Reverse-bidding auction matches you with empanelled developer. PPA signed (tripartite: you + developer + UPPCL).

Step 04 · Month 6-15

Plant construction

Developer builds, commissions, integrates with grid. You receive lease payments from Month 6 onwards.

Step 05 · Month 15+

Operational

Plant generating, UPPCL paying developer monthly. You receive lease + revenue share per PPA terms.

§ Watch out for

Component A red flags.

Component A involves long-duration contracts and high-value capex. Bad actors target landowners with promises that sound too good. If you see any of these, walk away or insist on UPNEDA verification before signing anything.

🚩 Refuse to sign if:

  • A developer asks you to pay any money upfront for "application fees," "site survey," or "connection charges." Legitimate Component A puts zero capex on you.
  • The lease term is less than 25 years (PPA standard) — anything shorter is a sign the developer is trying to flip the project before construction
  • No tripartite PPA at signing — the PPA between developer and UPPCL must be in place BEFORE you sign your lease
  • Developer promises returns significantly above ₹75K/acre/year — anything over that range is either a non-empanelled developer or a scam
  • Pressure to sign immediately without time to verify on UPNEDA portal
  • Lease terms hand over full title rather than usage rights — you retain ownership, period
  • No exit clause for developer default — what happens if they stop building? The PPA must specify recovery
§ Common questions

Component A FAQ.

Do I lose ownership of my land for 25 years?

No. You retain full land ownership. The developer gets a 25-year usage lease, not a transfer of title. At the end of 25 years, the plant is either decommissioned (your land returned to original state) or you renegotiate a new lease.

What if my land is partly cultivated, partly barren?

You can still apply for the barren portion as long as it's at least 4 acres contiguous. Mixed-use farms (some cultivated + some barren) are explicitly listed as eligible. UPNEDA will inspect to confirm which portions qualify.

What about land that's only seasonally usable?

If land is genuinely barren during 6+ months a year (post-harvest fallow, monsoon-only cultivation, salinity), it can qualify as "fallow" under Component A. Provide documentation of the limited-use pattern during application.

How does the substation proximity actually work?

The substation must be a 33/11 kV (sub-transmission) substation, NOT just a distribution transformer. Your local UPNEDA office can confirm the nearest qualifying substation. Distance is measured as transmission line distance, not as-the-crow-flies. If you're at 4.8 km but the road/right-of-way runs 6 km, you might not qualify.

Can I do Component A AND keep some land for farming?

Yes. The 4-16 acres for the plant is separate from your overall landholding. If you own 20 acres total, you can dedicate 4-8 acres to Component A and continue farming the remaining 12-16 acres. The plant just needs to be on contiguous barren land within 5 km of the substation.

What if my income from the plant is delayed by UPPCL payment issues?

This is a real risk in 2024-25. Some Bundelkhand farmers are seeing 60-90 day delays in lease payments. Two protections: (1) The PPA requires developer to pay you regardless of UPPCL's payment timing (the developer bears the cash-flow risk, not you). (2) Late-payment penalties accrue per the PPA. But practically, if the developer is being squeezed by UPPCL, they may pause payments to you too. Verify recent payment history with existing Component A farmers in your district before signing.

How does this compare to selling the land?

Math comparison: Selling 4 acres of barren UP land at typical ₹3-8 Lakh/acre = ₹12-32 Lakh one-time. Component A on the same 4 acres = ₹2.4 Lakh/year × 25 years = ₹60 Lakh (₹2.5x more), AND you keep ownership. The trade-off: you can't sell during the 25-year lease, and you're exposed to DISCOM payment delays. For most landowner-farmers with truly barren land, Component A is the financially smarter path.
