§ Honest assessment

Should you apply for KUSUM?

Most KUSUM blog content is written by vendors trying to sell you pumps. We're not. Here's who actually benefits, and who shouldn't bother.

✅ Strong fit if:

  • You own at least 0.5 acres of agricultural land
  • You have an identifiable water source (borewell, well, pond, or canal access)
  • Your current irrigation is rain-fed, diesel pump, or unreliable grid electricity
  • You have valid land records (RoR / Khasra-Khatauni) and an Aadhaar-linked bank account
  • You can put up the 10% own-funds portion (₹10K-₹60K depending on pump size) within 30 days of approval

⚠️ Probably not a fit if:

  • You're a tenant farmer without ownership of the land (KUSUM requires land ownership documents)
  • You already have reliable grid electricity for your pump (Component C might help but check carefully — sometimes the math doesn't work)
  • You can't identify a stable water source on your land
  • You already received a KUSUM subsidy in the past 5 years (anti-double-subsidy rule applies)

Why KUSUM is different from rooftop solar

PM Surya Ghar (rooftop) gets all the marketing attention because urban customers have higher willingness to pay. KUSUM is structurally more valuable for the right farmer — 60% subsidy vs ~40% on rooftop, with bank loan eligibility for another 30%. But the eligibility checks are different, vendor pool is different, and most agents don't understand the three components.

Our calculator handles all three. Component A (large land), Component B (off-grid pump), Component C (grid-connected pump). Different qualifying questions, different subsidy math, different timelines.

§ The math (Component B, 5 HP example)

What you'll actually pay.

Most common scenario: farmer with 3-5 acres, currently rain-fed or diesel-pumping, applies for 5 HP solar pump under Component B.

Pump cost (MNRE benchmark)₹3,05,000
Central subsidy (MNRE)− ₹91,500 (30%)
State subsidy (UPNEDA)− ₹91,500 (30%)
Your share₹1,22,000 (40%)
Bank loan (NABARD-channelled)₹91,500 (30%)
Your own funds (cash)₹30,500 (10%)

Of your ₹1,22,000 share, up to ₹91,500 (30% of total cost) can be a NABARD-channelled bank loan via PNB, SBI, or Bank of Baroda. Your minimum out-of-pocket: ₹30,500.
