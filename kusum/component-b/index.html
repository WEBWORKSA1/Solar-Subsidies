§ What it actually is

Component B in plain language.

A standalone solar pump is exactly that: a pump with its own solar panel array, completely off-grid. You install it once, and for the next 5-10 years it pumps water from your borewell, open well, or pond into your fields whenever the sun is up. No electricity bills. No diesel costs. No grid dependency.

The subsidy structure is straightforward: MNRE pays 30% of the benchmark pump cost, UPNEDA pays 30%, and you pay 40%. Of your 40%, up to 30% (of the total cost) can be a bank loan through NABARD-channelled banks. So your minimum out-of-pocket is roughly 10% of the total cost.

What's included in the install

  • Solar panel array (typically 1.2× the pump's HP-equivalent kWp — e.g. 6 kWp panels for a 5 HP pump)
  • Pump motor (BLDC submersible for borewells; surface pump for ponds/wells)
  • VFD (Variable Frequency Drive) controller — lets the pump operate at variable solar output
  • Mounting structure, cabling, civil work for panel installation
  • 5-year comprehensive maintenance contract (free parts + labour for 5 years)
Best part: You never pay UPNEDA directly. The subsidy is netted from the vendor's invoice. You only pay your 40% share to the vendor, who has already discounted the price by the 60% subsidy amount.
§ Eligibility

Who qualifies?

✅ You qualify if:

  • You own at least 0.5 acres of agricultural land
  • You have a water source on the land (borewell, open well, pond, or canal nearby)
  • You don't have a working grid electricity connection on the land (or it's unreliable)
  • You're currently using diesel, manual lift, rain-fed cropping, or no irrigation at all
  • You have land ownership documents (RoR / Khasra-Khatauni)
  • You haven't received a KUSUM subsidy in the past 5 years

⚠️ You don't qualify if:

  • You already have reliable grid electricity on the agricultural land (Component C might fit instead)
  • Your land is less than 0.5 acres (PM Surya Ghar rooftop solar might be a better fit)
  • You don't have an identifiable water source (drill a borewell first, then apply)
  • You're a tenant farmer or sharecropper without ownership rights
  • You've already received KUSUM-B subsidy in the past 5 years (anti-double-subsidy rule)

Pump capacity recommended by land + crop

  • 0.5–1 acre, vegetables/orchard: 1–2 HP pump
  • 1–2 acres, mixed cropping: 3 HP pump
  • 2–4 acres, paddy/sugarcane: 5 HP pump
  • 4–7 acres, deep borewell (>100 ft): 7.5 HP pump
  • 7+ acres, commercial farm or very deep borewell (>150 ft): 10 HP pump

§ Application process

From application to working pump.

Typical Component B timeline: 4 months from application to operational pump. Faster if your district has open UPNEDA quota; slower during peak sowing season (June-August).

Apply on PM-KUSUM portal or UPNEDA district officeOnline application at pmkusum.mnre.gov.in or in person at UPNEDA office. Choose pump capacity based on land size and crop.
Submit documentsAadhaar, land records (RoR / Khasra-Khatauni), bank account proof, water source proof (borewell certificate or geotagged photograph).
UPNEDA field verification (15–30 days)Officer visits your land, confirms ownership, water source presence, and no existing grid connection.
Sanction order + vendor allocation (60–90 days)Subsidy sanctioned in your name. UPNEDA assigns or you select an empanelled vendor from approved list. Bank loan applied for if needed.
Pay your 40% shareYou pay the vendor your share (30% loan + 10% own funds, or 40% cash). Subsidy is already netted from the invoice.
Installation (15–30 days post-payment)Vendor installs solar panels, pump, VFD, and tests system. UPNEDA officer signs off on commissioning.
5-year AMC beginsVendor is contractually responsible for maintenance, repairs, and replacement parts for 5 years from commissioning. No additional cost to you.
§ Red flags + watchouts

What to watch for.

🚩 Avoid vendors who:

  • Demand full payment upfront before subsidy approval. Legitimate vendors collect only your 40% share after the subsidy is sanctioned in your name.
  • Aren't on the UPNEDA empanelled list. Verify on upneda.org.in before signing anything. Subsidy doesn't reach non-empanelled vendors.
  • Quote significantly above the MNRE benchmark. Benchmark is fixed. If the vendor's quote is 20%+ above benchmark, walk away.
  • Pressure you to oversize the pump. A 10 HP pump on 2 acres is regulatory non-compliance — UPNEDA can revoke the subsidy. Buy the right size for your land.
  • Don't put 5-year AMC in writing. The 5-year maintenance is mandatory — if it's not in your written contract, it doesn't exist.
  • Promise installation "next week." Realistic timeline is 4 months. Anyone promising faster is either misrepresenting or working outside the official process.
If a vendor demands cash above your 40% share — leave immediately. Subsidy fraud is real. The subsidy belongs to you, not the vendor. If they ask for it back in any form (cash kickback, "processing fee," "facilitation charge"), report them to UPNEDA at 1800-180-3030.
