§ What is Component B?

How Component B works.

Component B replaces your diesel pump (or installs a new pump if you have none) with a standalone solar pump that runs entirely off solar panels. No grid electricity needed.

The pump is sized to match your farm — typically 1 HP for vegetable plots up to 10 HP for commercial sugarcane farms. Solar panels generate the electricity directly. Water flows during sunlight hours. Excess water can be stored in a tank for evening use.

The subsidy math is straightforward: MNRE-fixed benchmark cost per HP, 30% paid by MNRE (Central), 30% paid by UPNEDA (State), 40% paid by the farmer. Of the farmer's 40%, up to 30% can be a NABARD-channelled bank loan (PNB, SBI, BoB at 7–9% interest). Minimum out-of-pocket: 10% of the total cost in cash.

✅ Why this is the most popular KUSUM path

Component B works for the largest farmer segment: rain-fed agriculture, diesel-pump dependence, or unreliable grid supply (Bundelkhand, Terai foothills, off-grid pockets across UP). Total subsidy: 60%. Bank loan eligible for another 30%. Farmer's minimum cash outlay: just 10%. Plus a mandatory 5-year vendor maintenance contract.

How we recommend your HP

Land size is the primary driver, but two adjustments matter:

  • Crop type: Sugarcane and paddy are water-intensive — bump up one HP tier. Vegetables and orchards use less water — can drop down one tier.
  • Water table depth: Borewells below 100 ft require minimum 5 HP. Below 150 ft requires minimum 7.5 HP for adequate lift.
§ Eligibility

Do you qualify?

✅ Strong fit

  • You own at least 0.5 acres of agricultural land
  • You have an identifiable water source: borewell, well, pond, river, or canal
  • Your current irrigation is rain-fed, diesel pump, or unreliable grid electricity
  • You have valid land records (RoR / Khasra-Khatauni) in your name
  • You have an Aadhaar-linked bank account
  • You can put up 10% own funds (₹10K to ₹60K depending on pump size) within 30 days of approval

⛔ Not a fit

  • You're a tenant farmer without land ownership documents
  • You have less than 0.5 acres of agricultural land
  • You have no identifiable water source on your land
  • You already received a KUSUM-B subsidy in the past 5 years (anti-double-subsidy rule)
  • You have reliable grid electricity already (Component C is the better fit)
  • You can't cover even the 10% own funds portion
§ The math (5 HP example)

What you'll actually pay.

The most common scenario: farmer with 3–5 acres, currently rain-fed or diesel-pumping, applies for a 5 HP standalone solar pump.

Pump cost (MNRE benchmark)₹3,05,000
Central subsidy (MNRE 30%)− ₹91,500
State subsidy (UPNEDA 30%)− ₹91,500
Your share (40%)₹1,22,000
  → Bank loan eligible (30%)₹91,500
  → Your own funds (10%)₹30,500

Of your ₹1,22,000 share, up to ₹91,500 can be a NABARD-channelled bank loan via PNB, SBI, or Bank of Baroda at 7–9% interest over 5–7 years. Your minimum out-of-pocket: ₹30,500. Subsidy is paid directly to the vendor — you don't wait for any reimbursement.

✅ What you save by ditching diesel

A 5 HP diesel pump uses 1.5–2 litres of diesel per hour. At ~₹95/litre and 5 hours/day average, that's ₹14,000+ per month during peak irrigation season, or ₹70K–1L per year. A solar pump eliminates this entirely. Annual fuel savings alone pay back the farmer's ₹1.22L share in 1.5–2 years.

§ The process

From application to working pump.

Typical timeline: ~4 months from application to operational pump. Faster if UPNEDA quota slots are open in your district; slower during peak sowing season (June–August).

Step 01 · Day 1-7

Apply on UPNEDA portal

Submit land records, Aadhaar, bank details, water source proof on PM-KUSUM portal or via UPNEDA district office.

Step 02 · Day 7-30

Field verification

UPNEDA officer visits your land. Verifies size, water source, no existing grid pump. Documents site photos + GPS coordinates.

Step 03 · Day 30-90

Pump allocation

UPNEDA allocates pump from MNRE-approved vendor list. Reverse auction picks lowest-bid vendor. Farmer's loan approved if applicable.

Step 04 · Day 90-120

Installation

Vendor installs pump, solar array, integrates with water source. 5-year comprehensive maintenance contract activated.

Step 05 · Day 120+

Operational + AMC

Pump operational. Vendor responsible for free parts + labour for 5 years from commissioning date.

§ Red flags

What to watch for.

Component B is straightforward but has known scam patterns. Walk away from vendors with these red flags:

  • Vendor not on MNRE empanelled list. Only MNRE-empanelled pump manufacturers (Shakti Pumps, KSB India, Lubi, Greaves Cotton, Jain Irrigation, etc.) and their authorized installers can deliver KUSUM-B pumps. Verify on mnre.gov.in.
  • Asking for full payment upfront before subsidy approval. The subsidy is paid directly to the vendor by UPNEDA. You only pay your 40% share — and only after sanction order is issued. Anyone demanding full payment upfront is running a scam.
  • Pump capacity oversized for your landholding. Some vendors push 7.5 HP pumps on 2-acre farms because the commission is higher. UPNEDA can reject the application or claw back subsidy if the pump is unjustifiably oversized.
  • No 5-year warranty in the written contract. KUSUM mandates 5 years of free comprehensive maintenance. If it's not in writing, it doesn't exist. Get it in the contract before signing.
  • Vendor doesn't visit your site before quoting. A real vendor needs to see the borewell depth, panel area, water source distance before quoting. "Standard rate, no site visit needed" is a scam pattern.
  • Asks you to inflate the benchmark cost. Some vendors try to inflate the invoice to claim higher subsidy and split the excess with the farmer. This is fraud. UPNEDA audits randomly; if caught, both vendor and farmer face penalties + subsidy clawback.
