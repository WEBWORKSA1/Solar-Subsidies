§ Honest fit assessment

Is Component B right for you?

Component B is the most accessible KUSUM path. About 60% of UP farmers can qualify. It targets off-grid agriculture and unreliable-grid farmers — the people for whom solar pumps make the biggest difference.

✅ Strong fit if:

  • You own 0.5+ acres of agricultural land (RoR / Khasra-Khatauni proves ownership)
  • You currently use a diesel pump, manual lift, or rain-fed irrigation
  • OR your grid electricity is unreliable (frequent outages, can't run pump during sowing/peak season)
  • You have an identifiable water source — borewell, open well, pond, river, or canal
  • You can fund 10% own funds upfront (₹9,500-₹56,000 depending on pump HP)
  • You have a NABARD-eligible bank account (PNB, SBI, Bank of Baroda, Canara, regional rural banks)

⚠️ Not a fit if:

  • You're a tenant farmer without land ownership (Component B requires ownership documents)
  • You already have reliable grid-connected electric pump — Component C is your path, not B
  • You don't have an identified water source — drill a borewell first, then apply
  • Your land is under 0.5 acres — below KUSUM minimums
  • You already received Component B subsidy in past 5 years (anti-double-subsidy rule)

How to pick the right HP

  • Under 0.5 acres → 1 HP (vegetable plots, small orchards, drip irrigation)
  • 0.5-1 acre → 2 HP (small farms, light irrigation needs)
  • 1-2 acres → 3 HP (mixed cropping, moderate water needs)
  • 2-4 acres → 5 HP (sugarcane, paddy, wheat — most common)
  • 4-7 acres → 7.5 HP (larger farms, deeper borewells over 100 ft)
  • 7+ acres → 10 HP (commercial farms, very deep aquifers over 150 ft)

Adjustments: Water-intensive crops (sugarcane, paddy) bump you up one size. Vegetables/orchards bump you down one size. Water table below 100 ft requires minimum 5 HP regardless of land area. Our eligibility check applies all these adjustments automatically.

§ Application process

From application to working pump.

Total timeline: ~4 months from initial application to operational pump. Faster if your district has UPNEDA quota slots open; slower during peak sowing season (June-August) when applications surge.

Step 01 · Day 1-7

Apply + Vendor Match

Apply on PM-KUSUM portal or via empanelled vendor. We match you with an MNRE-pump vendor in your district.

Step 02 · Day 7-37

Documents + Field Verification

Submit Aadhaar, land records, bank details, water source proof. UPNEDA field officer visits (15-30 days).

Step 03 · Day 37-90

Sanction + Bank Loan

Subsidy sanction order from UPNEDA. Bank loan approval if applying (30-45 days separately).

Step 04 · Day 90-115

Pump Allocation

UPNEDA allocates pump from empanelled manufacturer (Shakti Pumps, KSB India, Lubi, Greaves Cotton).

Step 05 · Day 115-130

Installation

Vendor installs pump (15-30 days post-allocation). 5-year AMC begins. You pay your 40% share to vendor.

§ Watch out for

Component B red flags.

Component B is the most common KUSUM path, which means it attracts the most middlemen and dodgy operators. If you see any of these, walk away or insist on UPNEDA verification.

🚩 Refuse to sign if:

  • Vendor asks for full payment upfront before subsidy approval — you should only pay your 40% AFTER UPNEDA sanctions the subsidy
  • Vendor is not on the UPNEDA empanelled installer list (verify at upneda.org.in or call UPNEDA district office)
  • Vendor offers a pump significantly cheaper than benchmark cost — they're likely cutting corners on pump quality or VFD specifications
  • Pump capacity is oversized for your landholding (more than 1 HP per 1 acre is usually wasteful and may trigger regulatory issues)
  • No 5-year comprehensive AMC in writing — this is mandatory under KUSUM, don't sign without it
  • Vendor pressures you to not apply for the bank loan portion and instead pay full ₹1,22,000 cash — they're trying to skip NABARD documentation that protects you
  • Pump is from a non-BIS-certified manufacturer or uses non-BLDC motor — KUSUM mandates BIS + BLDC standards
§ Common questions

Component B FAQ.

I have a small piece of land (0.5 acres). Is 1 HP really enough?

For vegetable plots or drip-irrigation orchards, yes. A 1 HP surface pump can irrigate 0.5-1 acre of light-water crops with drip systems. If you grow water-intensive crops (paddy, sugarcane) even on a small plot, consider 2 HP instead — the marginal cost is only ₹4,300 more in own funds for double the capacity.

What if my borewell is very deep (over 150 ft)?

You need minimum 7.5 HP submersible pump for borewells over 150 ft. The deeper the water table, the more head pressure required, and a smaller pump will either fail to lift water or burn out under load. Our eligibility check automatically bumps you to 7.5 HP if you report a deep borewell — don't try to economise on pump size here.

Can I take a bank loan if I'm not a Kisan Credit Card holder?

Yes. NABARD-channelled banks (PNB, SBI, BoB, Canara, RRBs) offer KUSUM loans to any farmer with land documents + Aadhaar + bank account. Interest rates are slightly higher without KCC (typically 8-9% vs 7-8% with KCC). Tenure 5-7 years. The loan is secured against the pump itself (collateral) — no additional security required.

What happens if my pump breaks down within 5 years?

The vendor is contractually obligated to fix it free of cost. Component B installations come with a mandatory 5-year comprehensive AMC — covering parts + labour. If your pump fails, contact the installing vendor first. If they don't respond, escalate to UPNEDA district office. Vendors who fail to honour AMC lose their empanellment.

Can I get Component B AND PM Surya Ghar on my home?

Yes. They're entirely separate schemes with separate budgets. A farmer can install a KUSUM-B solar pump on the farm AND a PM Surya Ghar rooftop solar system on the farmhouse independently. The pump serves the farm load; the rooftop serves the household load. No double-counting.

I have an old electric pump. Can I scrap it and apply for Component B?

Not quite. If you have an existing grid-connected electric pump, you should apply for Component C (grid-pump solarisation) instead. Component B is specifically for farmers without grid-connected pumps. Trying to disconnect your existing pump to qualify for B would be flagged during UPNEDA field verification.

Does the pump come with a battery backup for night-time irrigation?

No. Standard KUSUM Component B pumps are direct solar-powered — they pump during sunlight hours only. The reasoning: solar pumps are designed to fill overhead storage tanks during the day; you use stored water for evening/night irrigation if needed. Battery-backed versions exist commercially but are not subsidised under KUSUM (the cost-benefit doesn't work — batteries add 40-50% to system cost for marginal benefit).

What's the lifespan of a KUSUM Component B pump?

Solar panels: 25-year manufacturer warranty (typically 80%+ output at year 25). Pump motor (BLDC submersible): 7-10 years lifespan, replaceable. VFD (Variable Frequency Drive): 5-7 years. Total system: 20-25 years usable life with one or two motor replacements over time. The 5-year AMC covers initial issues; after that, replacements are at your cost (a new BLDC motor runs ₹15,000-₹40,000 depending on HP).
