## Why KUSUM specifically

Most solar aggregators (Justdial, Sulekha, even Tata) don't differentiate KUSUM from rooftop. They blast farmer leads to rooftop installers who can't actually install solar pumps.

We have:
- A dedicated 6-step KUSUM eligibility flow (Component A/B/C-specific)
- Separate vendor pool flagged as `kusum_specialist=true` (MNRE pump empanellment required)
- Different lead-scoring logic (drought-district priority, water-source mandatory)
- Different routing: KUSUM leads only go to KUSUM-empanelled vendors

If you do KUSUM pumps (BLDC submersibles, VFD-integrated, 5-year AMC), this is a differentiated channel. PM-KUSUM disbursement in UP was under-target by ~40% in 2024-25 per MNRE — meaning farmers can't easily find capable vendors. We fix that.

## Common questions

**Q: How many vendors per district will you have?**
A: We don't blast leads. One lead = one vendor at a time. We aim for 2-4 vendors per district in active districts so leads have backup vendors for declines, but no lead is ever sent to multiple vendors simultaneously.

**Q: What if I get a lead that's a tire-kicker?**
A: Decline it in the portal with a reason. The lead reassigns to the next vendor. You're not penalized for declining — only for slow response on accepted leads.

**Q: What's your refund policy on commission?**
A: If a customer disputes the install or you pause the project before net-meter activation, no commission is owed. We waive any commission claim if the deal genuinely falls through after activation due to customer issues (e.g., they default on their loan).

**Q: Can I see your traffic data?**
A: Once you sign, yes — we'll share Google Analytics access read-only so you can see what's coming through your district. Before signing, we can show you general traffic patterns and the indexed sitemap.

**Q: Why should I sign now vs wait 6 months?**
A: First-cohort vendors get **probation tier at 7% commission**, with auto-promotion to **standard tier at 7%** after 10 closed deals. Vendors joining later start at probation too but with less brand familiarity and fewer leads (because districts will already have established vendors). Early signers also get input on roadmap features.
