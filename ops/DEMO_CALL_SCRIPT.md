# Vendor Demo Call Script — 15 Minutes

**Goal:** Close a verbal "send me the agreement" by end of call
**Format:** Video call (Google Meet / Zoom) — screen-share required
**Pre-call setup:** Have the platform open in browser, sample lead ready

---

## Pre-call checklist (5 min)

- [ ] Vendor's website tab open (scan their portfolio)
- [ ] Vendor's MNRE/UPNEDA status checked (note any flags)
- [ ] `/d/[their main district].html` open
- [ ] `/calculator.html` open in incognito (for live demo)
- [ ] `/admin/` dashboard open (logged in)
- [ ] `/vendors/portal.html` open in another tab (sample login)
- [ ] WhatsApp open on phone (to show notification example)
- [ ] Their company name + contact name written down (don't fumble names)

---

## Call Script

### Minute 0-1: Open

> "Hi [Name], thanks for jumping on this call. Can you hear me okay?"
> 
> [Wait for confirmation]
> 
> "I'll keep this to 15 minutes. Here's the agenda:
> 1. 2 minutes — what SolarSubsidies.com does
> 2. 8 minutes — quick platform walkthrough
> 3. 5 minutes — your questions + next steps
> 
> Sound good?"

### Minute 1-3: Quick context

> "SolarSubsidies.com launched in May 2026. UP-focused — 459 district and DISCOM pages indexed for searches like 'solar subsidy Lucknow' or 'PM Surya Ghar Varanasi.'
> 
> The customer journey: they land on our calculator, answer 4 questions about their bill, property type, intent, and timeline. We score them HOT, WARM, or COLD. HOT and WARM leads get auto-matched to one vendor in their district. You get a WhatsApp with full context — name, phone, bill amount, property type, system size, urgency.
> 
> You decide whether to take the lead. If you accept, you call the customer within 4 hours. If you close, we charge 7% commission paid net-30 after net meter activation. If you decline or the deal doesn't close, you pay nothing.
> 
> Different from Justdial — we don't sell the same lead to 5 installers. One lead = one vendor at a time. If you decline, it goes to the next-best fit.
> 
> Does this fit what you're looking for in a lead channel?"

[Listen. Note their reaction. Adjust pitch if needed.]

### Minute 3-11: Platform walkthrough

**Show the customer side first (2 minutes):**

> "Let me share my screen. This is what your future customer sees."
> 
> [Open `/d/[their district].html`]
> 
> "Every UP district has its own page. This is Lucknow's — DISCOM-specific tariff data, district-specific subsidy info, a calculator at the bottom. Indexed by Google, ranking for 'solar subsidy Lucknow' searches."
> 
> [Open `/calculator.html`]
> 
> "Customer clicks 'Calculate.' They answer 4 questions: intent, bill amount, property type, timeline. Watch what happens..."
> 
> [Fill in: HOT lead — ₹6500 bill, independent home, this month, cut bill]
> 
> "Result page shows them their system size, subsidy amount, payback. They submit their phone and we capture them as a HOT lead — score 9/10."

**Show the vendor side (4 minutes):**

> "Now your side — let me switch tabs."
> 
> [Open vendor portal]
> 
> "Vendor portal. Mobile-friendly. Your team logs in via WhatsApp OTP — no passwords. Session lasts 30 days."
> 
> [Show login screen]
> 
> "Once logged in, you see your inbox. Each card is a lead:"
> 
> [Show sample lead card if available, or describe]
> 
> "Each lead shows: customer name, phone, district, system size, monthly bill, intent, timeline, expected commission, SLA timer.
> 
> Three buttons: 'WhatsApp Customer' which pre-fills a message and opens WhatsApp deep-link; 'Update Status' which tracks the deal through site visit, quote, contract, install, net meter activation; 'Decline' which releases the lead back to the pool with a reason."

**Show admin tracking (2 minutes):**

> "On my side, I see everything you do. Every action is tracked. Coverage map shows which districts need more vendors. Commission ledger tracks every owed/invoiced/paid commission. Vendor health flags show me if any vendor is consistently slow to respond."
> 
> [Open admin coverage tab]
> 
> "Right now, [their district] is showing as GAP_HOT or SINGLE — meaning we either have no vendor or just one in the district. That's why I'm reaching out to you specifically. If you sign, you're either the first or second vendor in [district], which means every lead in your area goes to you for the first 90 days minimum."

**Show the WhatsApp notification (1 minute):**

> [Pull out phone, show example WhatsApp message you can mockup or have a real test message]
> 
> "This is what arrives on your phone when a lead comes in. Score, customer info, expected commission, claim link. Takes 10 seconds to decide."

### Minute 11-13: Address objections

Listen for these common ones and respond:

**"How many leads will I get?"**
> "Honest answer for [their district]: 3-8 per month within 90 days. Less in the first month while we onboard you. Could be more in Lucknow, less in Hardoi — depends on traffic to district pages. SEO takes 2-3 months to mature."

**"Why only 7%? Most aggregators charge 15-20%."**
> "Because our cost per lead is lower. We're SEO-first — once a page ranks, leads are essentially free to acquire. Justdial spends ₹3000+ per lead on Google Ads, then charges you ₹500+ per lead delivery. We don't have that variable cost, so we can charge less. Plus you only pay on close, not on lead delivery."

**"What if I sign and you don't deliver?"**
> "Two protections: (1) You pay zero until a lead actually closes and net meter activates. Worst case for you is wasted time evaluating leads — no money lost. (2) Agreement is month-to-month — no lock-in. If you don't see value in 90 days, you walk."

**"My competitors are listed too?"**
> "Yes, likely. But not at the same time. One lead = one vendor. The matching engine picks based on tier, capacity, and response time. If you respond fast and close well, you'll get more leads than competitors. Premium tier auto-promotion after 10 closes with 25% close rate, then your commission rate drops further or you get priority routing."

**"What's the catch?"**
> "Honest answer: SEO takes time. First 30-60 days will be slow because rankings haven't matured. If you sign expecting 10 leads in week 1, you'll be disappointed. If you sign with a 6-month horizon, you'll see meaningful volume by month 3-4. Be patient with the launch curve."

**"Why aren't you a registered MNRE empanelled aggregator yourself?"**
> "We're not a vendor — we're an editorial-style lead-gen platform. MNRE empanellment is for solar EPCs (you) and panel/inverter manufacturers. We're more like Justdial than Tata Power Solar. We curate, qualify, and route — you install."

### Minute 13-15: The ask

> "Okay, ground we've covered:
> - Pre-qualified leads in [district]
> - 7% commission, paid net-30 after net meter activation
> - No upfront fees, no exclusivity, decline anything that doesn't fit
> - Portal access for self-serve lead management
> - First-vendor advantage in [district] for 90 days
> 
> Does this make sense as something worth trying?"

**If YES:**
> "Great. Here's the verification I need from you:
> 1. Your MNRE empanellment number — I verify on mnre.gov.in (5 min check on my end)
> 2. Your UPNEDA approval number — same verification
> 3. Districts you actively serve (so we route correctly)
> 4. WhatsApp number checked daily
> 5. Bank account details for commission settlements (later, post-agreement)
> 
> Can you send those to me on WhatsApp today or tomorrow? Once verified, I'll send the agreement Friday."

[Get verbal commitment to send credentials. Don't end call without this.]

**If MAYBE / NEED TO THINK:**
> "Totally fair. What specifically would help you decide? Is it the volume question, the commission, or something else?"

[Address the specific concern. If unresolved:]

> "Here's a no-commitment option: send me your MNRE + UPNEDA numbers anyway. I'll verify them. If you decide to sign later, you skip 2-3 days of back-and-forth. If not, no harm done. Sound okay?"

**If NO:**
> "Fair enough. What was the dealbreaker for you? Volume, commission, or something else?"
> 
> [Listen carefully — this is feedback for your offer]
> 
> "Even if this isn't for you, do you know 1-2 other UP installers who might want pre-qualified leads? An intro would help us a lot."

### Minute 15: Close

> "Thanks for your time. If you have questions after this, WhatsApp me at [number]. I'll send a summary of what we discussed today within an hour, plus the verification request."
> 
> [End call]

## Red flags during call — when to walk

- Vendor asks "How do I know you'll actually pay commissions?" repeatedly → Trust concern. They likely won't sign even if you push. Don't push, move on.
- Vendor asks for exclusivity in their district → Decline politely. We don't do exclusivity. They're not a fit.
- Vendor wants you to pay them per lead (reversed model) → They don't understand the offer. Try to re-explain once. If they still don't get it, move on.
- Vendor reveals they're not MNRE empanelled → Disqualify. You can't onboard them.
- Vendor's website shows no installation portfolio or is just a Google Sites page → Probably a fly-by-night operator. Disqualify.
- Vendor pushes back on net-30 payment terms → Acceptable concern. Counter with net-15 if they've got 3+ years in business and clean GSTIN. Walk if they want upfront/COD.

---

## Green flags during call — push to close fast

- Vendor mentions they currently pay Justdial / Sulekha → They already accept "pay for leads" model. Easy yes.
- Vendor mentions specific UP district pain points → They actually operate there. Real prospect.
- Vendor asks detailed questions about lead quality, response time, scoring → Buying signal. They're imagining real workflow.
- Vendor mentions a recent install and shows pride → Real installer with real portfolio.
- Vendor has been in solar 5+ years → Established business, low fly-by-night risk.

For green-flag vendors: try to get verbal "yes, send the agreement" before ending the call. Don't let them go cold.

---

## When to skip the demo entirely

If a vendor sends WhatsApp saying:
> "Sounds good, send me the agreement"

...without asking to see the platform first, **don't insist on a demo**. They've already mentally signed. Send the verification request immediately:

```
Great. Before I send the agreement, I need to verify two things (takes me 5 min):
1. Your MNRE empanellment number
2. Your UPNEDA approval number

Please share both. Once verified, agreement goes out by [tomorrow date].
```

Then send agreement same day after verification.

Speed kills friction. The faster you can move from "interested" to "signed," the higher your conversion rate.
